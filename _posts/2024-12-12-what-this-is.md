In short, this is what it is and it's not what it's not. In other words, I don't want to pigeonhole this into a specific category, although it may end up following some common theme(s). I don't know. We'll have to wait and see.

For now, I can tell you what I currently want to write about. I would like to share things from physics and math which I find interesting and/or useful. This will probably end up being a mix of elementary and advanced topics, but of course the difficulty level is subjective.

I would be really happy if someone found some entertainment and/or learned something new here but I also have selfish reasons for writing: this will help me organize my thoughts on certain ideas and force me to be clear and correct with the ultimate goal of clearing up any confusions I may have and having references I can return to in the future. Terence Tao has a great [post](https://terrytao.wordpress.com/career-advice/write-down-what-youve-done/) on this that resonates with me.

I expect that some posts will be more useful as references and others will prioritize pedagogy. Also, I might do this for 2 weeks then abandon it. Or maybe I'll post a bunch in the first few months then post once a year. Again, we'll see how it goes.

I should make a note on rigor. You should expect rigor in the flavor of a physicist or engineer rather than a mathematician. I want to be able to use the Dirac delta without invoking Schwartz' work that put it on rigorous grounding; if it was good enough for Dirac to use before Schwartz, it's good enough for me. I want to be able to exchange integrals and derivatives without anxiety. I do not want to spend time proving the conditions for the dominated convergence theorem hold before switching limits and integrals&mdash;I'm sorry, but I have a finite lifespan. Maybe every now and then I'll throw in something like that but yes, some combination of me finding it unnecessary, boring, or overly time-consuming will prevent those kinds of arguments appearing here for the most part. I'll share a relevant quote from the preface of Anthony Zee's quantum field theory book: 

<blockquote>
  <p>Indeed, a Fields Medalist once told me that top mathematicians secretly think like physicists and after they work out the broad outline
of a proof they then dress it up with epsilons and deltas [...] As we like to say in the theoretical physics community, too much rigor soon leads to rigor mortis. Be warned.</p> 

  <footer>— Anthony Zee, <em>Quantum Field Theory in a Nutshell,</em> 2nd Edition</footer>
</blockquote>

Having said that, I will strive to not be egregiously hand-wavy either. For any steps that I am not completely comfortable with, I will be honest and point those out as they arise instead of pretending hand-waviness makes sense. Consider those occurrences as invitations to enlighten me.

Anyway, as I said at the start, I want to be able to talk about a variety of topics here. So even if I begin with a bunch of physics and math, do not be surprised by movie recs, rants on American media and car centricity, showerthoughts, etc..
